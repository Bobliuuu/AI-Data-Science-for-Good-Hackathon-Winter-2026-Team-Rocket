# design

Rules: Keep the UI clean, warm, and minimal. Do not overpopulate `globals.css`. Do not add unnecessary effects or clutter. Prioritize a mobile-first layout. Assume light mode only for the first version.

# styling

Use a soft, modern mobile app style with a beige color scheme:
- warm beige or light cream backgrounds
- white or cream cards
- large rounded corners
- subtle shadows
- dark charcoal text instead of pure black
- warm yellow as the main accent
- muted pastel secondary accents

If possible, do not use any text. If there is text, typography should feel friendly and readable. Use clear hierarchy and larger text for important phrases and actions.

# layout

Design for simple, low-stress interaction:
- one main goal per screen
- generous spacing
- large tappable cards and buttons
- minimal text
- clear visual hierarchy
- avoid dense dashboards and cluttered menus

# components

Use:
- rounded scenario cards
- large phrase suggestion buttons
- simple bottom navigation
- clear CTA buttons
- audio and microphone buttons that feel large and easy to tap
- category chips for common situations like school, doctor, store, transit, and work

# imagery

Use simple flat illustrations or soft icons to quickly show context. Keep visuals warm, friendly, and non-distracting.

# microinteractions

hover: slightly brighten the element and increase shadow subtly  
expand: slightly scale cards or buttons on hover/focus  
press: slightly scale down on tap  
transition: keep motion smooth, soft, and minimal

# overall feel

The app should feel supportive, calm, welcoming, and easy to use. It should look like a polished modern language-support app focused on helping users speak confidently in real-world situations.
